Audio Imitator: Controlling Timbre and Prosody in Video2Audio Synthesis with Audio Reference

Overview

Video-to-audio (V2A) generation has recently attracted increasing attention, particularly in achieving semantic consistency and temporal alignment. Existing works have demonstrated promising progress in this direction, but they generally condition only on visual/text content and provide little control over timbre and prosody. Timbre refers to voiceprint-like characteristics, while prosody refers to the rhythm and temporal dynamics. We introduce AudioIM, a promptable video-to-audio generator built on the MMAudio backbone with two key extensions: (i) a temporal-masking strategy during training that enables the model to leverage an audio prompt, and (ii) dual style encoders that inject prompt-derived features into both global and frame-level conditioning, thereby allowing fine-grained control of timbre and prosody. AudioIM produces audio with higher style similarity to the prompt and achieves strong performance on core video-to-audio tasks, while preserving semantic and temporal alignment.





Figure 1. Overview of AudioIM. The training mask and the style encoder of the proposed method. Through mask training, the model is enabled to leverage prompt audio latents to provide fine-grained stylistic information for audio generation. We separately extract timbre and prosody features from the prompt audio by two encoders, and subsequently fuse them and inject fused feature into the model through conditioning.

Although existing methods can accomplish this task through multi-round inference, the generated segments are independent of each other, resulting in noticeable unnaturalness and abrupt transitions. In contrast, our method produces speech with smooth intra-sentence emotional shifts and speaking rate variations that sound highly natural.
This serves as the primary evaluation task in our paper, as the baseline performs very poorly on the first cross-speaker task.
